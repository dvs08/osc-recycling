Get involved with Recycling and Sustainability

A service at The Overseas School of Colombo to reduce carbon footprint

The Overseas School of Colombo (OSC) provides many service-learning activities, groups, and classes. One of these services is Recycling and Sustainability which is one of the oldest services running at OSC. One thing this Recycling and Sustainability does is collecting recyclable waste, such as paper, cardboard etc. from the school buildings and sending it out to a recycling center. This service also advocates for OSC to become a greener school and reduce our ecological footprint. Over the history of the service a few things we have done are listed below.

Installed a biogas plant to convert food waste into usable gas, which can be used for cooking. This idea was initially proposed and setup by Disara Samayawardhena in 10th grade in 2017-18. From the use of the fuel from this biogas plant the members of Recycling and Sustainability have been cooking some delicious popcorn recently using the biogas plant and distributing among service groups.

Recycling and Sustainability has drastically reduced the plastic usage at the canteen from their projects and aims. They did this by removing disposable plastic utensils, plates and cups and replacing them with reusable versions. Along with previous IB Diploma student’s CAS projects such as providing a 4rth bin for waste separation.

This service plans to create many initiatives for OSC and continue with our already made initiatives.

For more in detail with the everyday work in Recycling and Sustainability:

Recycling and Sustainability is one of the two services that focus on the environment's wellbeing. The service also works on reducing the school’s carbon footprint. At the start of every service session we meet up at Mr. Lockwood’s classroom, which is our service class room, and then split in groups to do collect recycling waste (mainly paper) from different buildings on the campus such as M Block, S Block, C Block, Primary block, Library, Art room, PE rooms, and Administration building and then begin the collection. The collection usually on average takes around 15 to 20 minutes. Since the recycling center also has a ‘plastic bottle’ collection area, the groups while collecting recycling paper also collect plastic bottles and deposit into the plastic bottle area. Once the collection is all done the collected recycling paper is brought to the Recycling shed/center and leave the collected paper, and cardboard in that room in separate bins and bags.

But those don’t stay there for long. Every week all the recycling paper and cardboard collected are put in a truck that gets sent to a recycling center nearby the school to sell those papers along with a group of 3 or 4 to negotiate with the scarp dealer with price rates and obtain to a new experience. During that time the 3 or 4 students that volunteered help also help in unloading the truck. They then put those papers the platform and weigh the weight, (cardboard and paper are separate). Then those are sold to the recycling center. The money that was earned from these short trips are used to organize events and fundraisers that are made by the Recycling and Sustainability service group. Recycling and Sustainability is held every Thursday from 2:50 to 4:00 PM and is offered in both school semesters. IB Diploma students tend to enroll in this service for the entire year where else students from 6th to 10th grade have choice to enroll for 1 semester or both. In the past and currently Recycling and Sustainability service group had many middle school students enrolling in both semesters and working towards reducing the carbon footprint at the school campus.
